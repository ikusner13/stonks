What "drift" means and why it matters

Drift is the gap between what you actually hold today and the allocation the optimizer suggests. As prices move, winners grow into a bigger slice of your portfolio and losers shrink — so your real risk slowly drifts away from your intended plan. Rebalancing (trimming the overweight, topping up the underweight) nudges you back toward target. These are prompts to consider, not instructions — you decide every trade.
