What "correlation" means and why it matters

Correlation measures how two stocks' day-to-day moves line up: near +1 they rise and fall together, near 0 they move independently. Owning ten names that all behave the same way isn't really ten bets — it's closer to one. That's "hidden" concentration the per-stock weights above can't reveal. When you weigh your next trade, adding something uncorrelated to what you already hold diversifies more than adding another name that moves with the pack.
