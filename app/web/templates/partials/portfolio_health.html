Why concentration matters for your next trade

Concentration is how much of your money sits in a few positions. Higher concentration increases the impact if one stock moves sharply against you — a single bad day in your largest holding can sink the whole portfolio. Before adding to a position you already hold, check whether it would push your top holdings even higher; spreading new money across less-concentrated names is usually the lower-risk choice.
